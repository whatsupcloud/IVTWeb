Security

Security commitments, operational safeguards, and responsible handling practices

Effective date: March 11, 2023. This page outlines the security-oriented practices adopted by Involytics Technology for the protection of its website, systems, and information assets. It is intended to provide a high-level overview and does not disclose confidential internal security details.

1. Security Approach

We aim to maintain a practical, risk-based security posture appropriate to the nature and scale of our operations. Security considerations may be incorporated into website administration, service delivery, development practices, cloud usage, access management, incident review, vendor selection, backup processes, and ongoing operational decision-making.

2. Administrative, Technical, and Organisational Controls

Depending on the nature of the system or project, our safeguards may include role-based access restriction, credential and password controls, principle-of-least-privilege access, environment separation where feasible, secure configuration practices, logging and monitoring, software updates, patch review, controlled sharing of sensitive information, approved tool usage, and periodic internal review of operational processes.

3. Secure Development and Change Practices

Where we design, develop, or manage software assets, we aim to follow reasonable development hygiene, including version control, controlled change implementation, dependency awareness, environment separation where feasible, and testing or review appropriate to the nature of the deliverable. Security review depth may vary depending on project scope, client requirements, deployment architecture, and service tier.

4. Infrastructure and Hosting Considerations

Our website and projects may rely on third-party hosting, cloud infrastructure, code repositories, communication tools, and managed services. We aim to select tools and providers appropriate for the intended use case and to configure them responsibly. However, some elements of availability, resilience, and infrastructure security may depend on third-party service providers outside our direct control.

5. Data Protection and Access Control

Access to business and project data is intended to be limited to authorised persons who need that access for operational, support, delivery, or compliance purposes. We aim to restrict unnecessary exposure of confidential or personal data and to follow reasonable internal discipline regarding sharing, storage, and handling of sensitive information.

6. Monitoring, Logging, and Security Review

We may use logs, technical monitoring, and system review practices to support troubleshooting, misuse detection, capacity assessment, and security visibility. Log data may be retained for periods reasonably necessary for system reliability, incident investigation, evidentiary needs, or compliance. Monitoring practices are implemented in line with operational necessity and available infrastructure.

7. Incident Management

In the event of a suspected or confirmed security issue, we may investigate the event, contain affected systems, preserve relevant logs or records, engage technical support providers or advisors, and take appropriate remediation measures. Where notification or reporting is required under applicable law, contract, or regulatory direction, such action may be undertaken in accordance with those obligations and the facts available at the time.

8. Vendor and Third-Party Risk

Our operations may involve third-party vendors, software services, hosting providers, communication platforms, or integrations. While we aim to use reputable providers and reasonable contractual or operational safeguards, we do not control all aspects of third-party security and cannot guarantee the security posture of external platforms. Vendor dependencies may affect overall security outcomes.

9. Business Continuity and Backups

Where appropriate to the system or project, we may maintain backups, version histories, or recovery-oriented mechanisms intended to support continuity, restoration, or issue recovery. Backup scope, frequency, and retention may vary depending on the type of project, contractual arrangement, cost profile, and operational need.

10. Responsible Disclosure

If you believe you have identified a genuine security vulnerability affecting our website or directly managed assets, please report it responsibly to inovalyticstechnology@gmail.com with sufficient detail to enable review. You must not exploit a suspected issue, access non-public data, disrupt services, or conduct intrusive testing without prior written authorization. We reserve the right to determine how reported issues are assessed, prioritised, and addressed.

11. User Responsibilities

Users of this website and our services are expected to maintain the security of their own devices, browsers, credentials, networks, and internal systems. We are not responsible for compromise caused by weak client-side security, credential leakage, phishing, malware on user devices, or unauthorised access resulting from the acts or omissions of the user or third-party systems under the user’s control.

12. No Absolute Guarantee

Although we apply reasonable efforts to maintain security, no online service, software environment, hosting stack, or communication channel can be guaranteed to be fully secure, continuously available, or immune from unauthorised activity. This Security page is informational in nature and does not create a contractual warranty, representation of certification, or unconditional guarantee of security performance.

13. Changes to This Page

We may revise this Security page from time to time to reflect changes in our operational practices, legal environment, website architecture, third-party infrastructure, or service model. The latest version published on this website will reflect our current high-level security communication approach.
