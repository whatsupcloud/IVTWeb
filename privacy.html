Privacy Policy

How Involytics Technology collects, uses, discloses, stores, and safeguards personal data

Effective date: March 11, 2023. This Privacy Policy describes the framework followed by Involytics Technology for handling digital personal data collected through this website and in connection with business enquiries, proposals, project execution, support, and related professional communications.

1. Introduction and Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to visitors to our website, prospective clients, current clients, vendors, collaborators, and other individuals who interact with Involytics Technology through our website, forms, emails, proposals, meetings, project discussions, and service delivery channels. It explains how we collect, use, disclose, retain, and protect personal data. This policy should be read together with any proposal, statement of work, services agreement, non-disclosure agreement, or project-specific contract entered into with us.

2. Applicable Legal Framework

We aim to process personal data in a manner consistent with applicable Indian legal and regulatory requirements, including the Information Technology Act, 2000, the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, relevant rules and subordinate legislation as applicable, and lawful cybersecurity and reporting obligations that may arise in connection with system protection, incident response, or regulatory compliance.

3. Categories of Personal Data We May Collect

Depending on the nature of the interaction, we may collect your name, business name, organisation details, job title, email address, phone number, project requirements, service interests, billing or contractual records, communications exchanged with us, support requests, and limited technical data such as IP address, browser type, operating system, timestamps, user-agent details, referring URLs, and security logs generated during website access or use of our systems.

4. Sources of Data

We may collect data directly from you when you submit a form, send an email, request a proposal, book a consultation, provide onboarding information, or communicate with our team. We may also receive information from your authorised representatives, publicly available business sources, referral channels, service providers acting on your instructions, or technical systems that automatically generate security and usage logs.

5. Purpose of Processing

We may process personal data for legitimate business and operational purposes including responding to enquiries, assessing service fit, preparing quotations or proposals, entering into and performing contracts, onboarding clients, delivering services, administering projects, sending notices, issuing invoices, maintaining records, improving website usability, protecting infrastructure, preventing unauthorised activity, meeting audit requirements, and complying with legal, regulatory, tax, or law-enforcement obligations.

6. Lawful Basis and Consent

Where applicable, we process personal data on the basis of consent, contractual necessity, compliance with legal obligations, protection of lawful business interests, security requirements, fraud prevention, or other grounds permitted by applicable law. By voluntarily submitting information through our website or by directly engaging with us for business purposes, you acknowledge and, where required, consent to such processing for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy.

7. Communications and Service Delivery

We may use your information to communicate regarding meetings, project discussions, proposals, scope clarifications, timelines, change requests, milestones, payments, support issues, security notices, policy updates, and other necessary business matters. These communications may be sent through email, phone, messaging tools, meeting platforms, project management systems, or similar channels used during a professional engagement.

8. Disclosure to Service Providers and Professional Advisors

We may disclose personal data to employees, contractors, technology partners, hosting providers, cloud service providers, communication vendors, analytics or security vendors, consultants, accountants, auditors, lawyers, or other trusted service providers who need the information to support our operations or to perform services on our behalf. Such disclosures are limited to what is reasonably necessary and are subject to confidentiality, security, and access-control expectations appropriate to the role of the receiving party.

9. Regulatory and Legal Disclosure

We may disclose information where disclosure is reasonably necessary to comply with applicable law, court order, legal process, government request, regulatory direction, cybersecurity incident response obligation, fraud-prevention requirement, or to establish, exercise, or defend legal rights. We may also disclose information to protect the integrity, security, and availability of our systems, services, team members, users, and business operations.

10. International or Cross-Border Processing

Some infrastructure, software tools, communication platforms, or backup services used in our operations may be hosted in India or other jurisdictions. Where cross-border processing occurs, we aim to use reputable providers and reasonable safeguards. However, data protection standards may vary by jurisdiction, and use of our services acknowledges this operational possibility to the extent legally permitted.

11. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, including enquiry management, contract performance, project records, internal administration, dispute resolution, fraud prevention, system protection, evidentiary needs, and compliance with legal or regulatory record-retention obligations. Retention duration may differ depending on the type of record and the nature of the relationship.

12. Security Measures

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, unlawful destruction, misuse, disclosure, alteration, and disruption. Such measures may include access restrictions, credential controls, principle-of-least-privilege practices, software updates, secure backups, monitoring, logging, and review of operational processes. Despite reasonable efforts, no system can be guaranteed to be fully secure.

13. Data Accuracy

We rely on the information provided by you or your authorised representatives. You are requested to ensure that the information you submit is accurate, current, complete, and lawfully shared. If your information changes, you should notify us so that records may be updated where necessary.

14. Cookies, Logs, and Technical Data

Our website may use essential technical features, server logs, and similar mechanisms for session continuity, website security, debugging, analytics readiness, traffic understanding, and performance management. For additional details regarding cookie-related technologies, please review our Cookie Policy.

15. Rights of Individuals

Subject to applicable law, you may be entitled to request access to personal data we hold about you, correction or updating of inaccurate information, withdrawal of consent where processing is based on consent, erasure of information where legally permissible, and grievance review in connection with our data handling practices. We may request reasonable identity verification and additional information before acting on a request.

16. Children's Data

Our website and services are intended for professional and business use and are not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children in violation of applicable law. If you believe information relating to a child has been provided to us inappropriately, please contact us so we may review the issue and take appropriate action.

17. Third-Party Websites and Services

Our website may contain references or links to third-party websites, applications, communication tools, or external platforms. We do not control the privacy practices of such third parties and are not responsible for their content, availability, or independent data processing practices. You should review the policies of any third-party service you use in connection with our website or services.

18. Grievance, Queries, and Contact

If you have a privacy-related question, request, concern, or grievance, you may contact Involytics Technology at inovalyticstechnology@gmail.com. We will review requests in accordance with applicable law, available records, operational feasibility, and identity-verification requirements.

19. Amendments to This Policy

We may revise this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in law, regulation, business practices, security requirements, vendor arrangements, or service delivery models. The updated version published on this website, together with its effective date, will govern our current practices from the date of publication unless a different date is expressly stated.
