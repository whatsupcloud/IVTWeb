Privacy Policy

How Involytics Technology collects, uses, and protects personal data

Effective date: March 11, 2026. This Privacy Policy describes the manner in which Involytics Technology processes digital personal data through this website and in connection with business enquiries, proposals, and service delivery.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to personal data collected through our website, contact forms, email communications, discovery calls, business proposals, and related service interactions. It is intended to be read with any separate proposal, statement of work, master services agreement, non-disclosure agreement, or other contract executed with a client.

2. Categories of Data We May Collect

Depending on your interaction with us, we may collect your name, business name, designation, email address, phone number, project requirements, billing or contractual information, communication records, and limited technical data such as IP address, browser type, device information, timestamps, and website usage logs generated for security and operational purposes.

3. Legal Basis and Purpose of Processing

We process personal data for lawful purposes including responding to enquiries, evaluating project requirements, preparing proposals, communicating with prospective or existing clients, performing contracts, improving our website and service quality, securing systems, maintaining records, and complying with applicable legal or regulatory obligations. Where required, we rely on consent or other lawful grounds available under applicable Indian law.

4. Use of Data

Your information may be used to contact you, schedule consultations, assess engagement suitability, provide services, manage projects, send invoices or commercial documents, administer accounts, investigate misuse, and maintain internal business records. We do not sell personal data.

5. Sharing and Disclosure

We may share information with employees, contractors, hosting providers, cloud or communication vendors, payment or compliance advisors, and other service providers strictly on a need-to-know basis and subject to appropriate confidentiality and security obligations. We may also disclose information when required by law, legal process, regulatory direction, or to protect our rights, systems, users, or operations.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including enquiry management, project delivery, audit requirements, legal compliance, dispute resolution, fraud prevention, and record-keeping obligations. Retention periods may vary depending on the nature of the relationship and applicable law.

7. Security Measures

We implement reasonable technical, administrative, and organizational safeguards designed to protect personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, loss, or misuse. However, no method of electronic transmission or storage can be guaranteed to be completely secure, and we cannot warrant absolute security.

8. Cookies and Technical Logs

This website may use essential technical mechanisms and server-side logs necessary for functionality, security, troubleshooting, and performance monitoring. If analytics, cookies, or similar technologies are introduced in the future, this Privacy Policy may be updated accordingly.

9. Children's Data

Our website and services are intended for business and professional use and are not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children in violation of applicable law. If you believe a child has provided personal data to us, please contact us so that appropriate action can be taken.

10. Your Rights and Requests

Subject to applicable law, you may request access to personal data we hold about you, correction or updating of inaccurate information, withdrawal of consent where processing is based on consent, erasure of data where legally permissible, and details of grievance redressal. We may request reasonable information to verify identity before acting on a request.

11. Grievance and Contact

For privacy-related requests, grievances, or questions regarding this Privacy Policy, you may contact us at inovalyticstechnology@gmail.com. We will review and respond in accordance with applicable law and our internal processes.

12. Cross-Border and Third-Party Processing

Where operationally necessary and legally permissible, certain service providers may process data on our behalf using infrastructure located in India or other jurisdictions. In such cases, we take reasonable steps to ensure that appropriate contractual and security protections are in place, subject to applicable legal restrictions.

13. Policy Changes

We may amend this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect legal, operational, technical, or business developments. The revised version published on this website, along with its effective date, will govern our current data handling practices.
